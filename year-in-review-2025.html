Climate Action in Our Hands

Regreening Northern Kenya

Mobilized the community for World Environment Day, National Tree Planting Day, and our dedicated regreening campaign, planting over 150 trees in partnership with Isiolo Scouts, Rovers, and Journey for Hope.

'Light in the Darkness'

Distributed 300 solar lanterns in Gotu, replacing hazardous kerosene lamps and providing families with safe, clean light for studying and evening activities.

"He who plants a tree, plants a hope."

Community-Led Regreening

Over 150 trees planted in partnership with local scouts and community organizations.

'The Land Is Our Mother'

Shifting National Dialogue

Co-led the session "Shifting Power, Voice and Agency to Grassroot Women in Land Governance" at the National Land Commission's conference, ensuring our voices were central to Kenya's land reform.

Indigenous Healing Circles

Launched 'Laaf Haadha Teenna' (The Land Is Our Mother), a groundbreaking initiative using cultural healing to address trauma and empower women to claim their land rights.

"The fight for women's land rights is a fight for justice, resilience, and sustainable development for grassroots women all over the world."

Women in Land Governance

Amplifying grassroots women's voices in national land policy discussions.

The Fire of Young African Feminists (COP30)

Challenging Exclusion at COP30

Joined a vital conversation to raise awareness about the barriers (visas, costs, access) that prevent young African feminists from participating in global climate negotiations.

Building Sisterhood & Strategy

Fostered networks of support among young women activists, proving that collective action can overcome systemic obstacles.

"We may not have all the access, but we have the fire and each other. Our voices are part of the climate story and without us, there is no justice."

Youth Climate Advocacy

Young African feminists demanding inclusion in global climate negotiations.

Cooperatives & Economic Empowerment

Unlocking Evening Potential

The 300 solar lanterns provided to the "Sons of Gold" community in Gotu extend the workday, allowing artisans to continue their craft and children to study without interruption.

Reducing Financial Burdens

By replacing expensive and inefficient kerosene, these lanterns free up household income, directly contributing to the economic resilience of pastoralist families.

Building a Foundation for Growth

These initiatives serve as a foundation for developing community cooperatives and strengthening local economies from the ground up.

Community Events & Partnerships

Local Action, Collective Strength

Our tree planting events were made possible by strong local partnerships with Isiolo Scouts, Isiolo Rovers, Journey For Hope, NIDO, and Move North.

Global Exchange & Learning

Our team's cultural immersion in Sweden allowed us to connect with new perspectives, share our story on an international stage, and build lasting global partnerships.

Impact Story: Light, Dignity, and Hope in Gotu

For hundreds of pastoralist families in the 'Sons of Gold' community, nightfall brought challenges: the risk of animal encounters, the danger of kerosene, and the end of the day's productivity. By providing 300 solar lanterns, we provided:

  • Safety: Protecting families from harm after dark.
  • Opportunity: Allowing children to study and artisans to work in the evenings.
  • Dignity: Delivering a clean, sustainable solution that honors their way of life and builds daily resilience.
